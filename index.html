What is Curandera

Tea as a ritual practice.

Curandera is a ritual practice, expressed through tea.

Rooted in the tradition of the curandera—a woman who heals through plants, through intuition, through deep listening.

This is not something to become. It is something within you.

Philosophy

Before the noise, there was knowing.

It lived in the body, in the hands—carried through generations, in women who understood how to listen.

To the earth. To plants. To what could not be explained, only felt.

Curandera is an ode to that knowing—not something new, but something you already carry.

The ritual

Steep. Breathe. Return.

Curandera is practiced through tea—a ritual of water, heat, and time.

Infused with plants long used for healing—each blend a quiet guide back to the body.

A moment to slow down, to listen inward.

Nothing added. Nothing forced.

The blends

Each one created to support a different way of returning.

Root

To return to what holds you.

Tulsi, roasted dandelion root, cinnamon, ginger, licorice root.

Open

To create space within.

Peppermint, lemongrass, lemon balm, nettle leaf, fennel seed.

Rest

To soften into yourself.

Chamomile, passionflower, rose petals, lavender, oatstraw.
